About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Biomedical Sciences with a strong interest in understanding how cognitive, emotional, and social processes emerge and change across development. My research combines behavioral methods and neuroimaging techniques to investigate working memory. I am particularly interested in how developmental changes in brain function contribute to individual differences in cognition and social behavior. Beyond research, I enjoy learning new analytical methods, developing reproducible workflows, and collaborating across disciplines. I believe that integrating perspectives from psychology, neuroscience, and statistics can provide deeper insights into human development.
